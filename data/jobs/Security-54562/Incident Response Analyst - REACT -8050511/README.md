<h3><strong>About the Role</strong></h3>
<p>Cloudflare is a system spanning the globe, on a mission to make the Internet safer and more powerful every day. To help fulfill this mission, we are seeking an incident response Analyst / Consultant to join our Cloudforce One REACT organization.</p>
<p>In this role, you will respond to active customer attacks across two primary tracks:<br>1) L7 mitigations — bot abuse, web scraping, credential stuffing, and WAF/API abuse.<br>2) L3/L4 mitigations — volumetric and protocol DDoS attacks.</p>
<p>You will execute hands-on edge mitigations, verify effectiveness with measurable KPIs, and communicate clearly with customers under pressure. This position requires an innovative, self-starting, detail-oriented problem solver with a passion for scoping, deploying, and validating mitigations in real time. You will engage with customers at all levels — including Executive, VP, Director, and engineering, serving an integral role alongside forensic analysts, threat researchers, detection engineers, and malware analysts.</p>
<p><strong>What You’ll Do</strong></p>
<p><br><strong>1. Active Edge Mitigation</strong><br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Deploy and tune custom WAF rules (block, challenge, JS challenge, managed challenge) to stop abusive L7 traffic.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Implement bot mitigations using request/behavior signals and, where applicable, fingerprinting signals; apply rate limiting and challenge policies.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Execute L3/L4 DDoS mitigations (e.g., attack mode tuning, shunning, flow-based controls) to protect customer availability before traffic reaches the origin.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Scope every mitigation tightly by path, endpoint, tenant, or audience to minimize collateral impact.</p>
<p><strong>2. Customer Containment &amp; Threat Isolation</strong><br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Identify and isolate infected hosts, revoke compromised sessions/identities, and stop data exfiltration when needed.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Track unauthorized lateral movement, correlate threat actor activity, and execute containment that preserves evidence while neutralizing the adversary.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Adapt mitigations as attacker tactics change without over-scoping or breaking legitimate traffic.</p>
<p><strong>3. Verification, KPIs &amp; Rollback</strong><br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Define “mitigated” using success criteria: service health recovery, reduced abusive match rate, and acceptable collateral limits.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Verify effectiveness after each mitigation update using time-bucketed telemetry; rollback or re-scope immediately if collateral damage appears.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Maintain a versioning mindset for mitigations so updates are reversible and auditable.</p>
<p><strong>4. IR Lifecycle, Evidence &amp; Reporting</strong><br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Support the full incident response lifecycle: investigation, containment, remediation, and recovery.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Preserve forensic evidence (logs, volatile memory, disk images) according to standards to support legal, regulatory, or insurance requirements.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Prepare high-fidelity incident reports and customer communications that are clear, accurate, and actionable for both executives and engineers.</p>
<p><strong>Minimum Qualifications (Must-Haves)</strong><br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• 3+ years of hands-on experience in SOC, incident response, or detection engineering.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Proven experience deploying mitigations that affect live customer traffic — not only monitoring or investigation.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Hands-on with at least one of the following: WAF rules/actions, DDoS protection controls, bot mitigations, rate limiting/challenge policies.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Strong incident process discipline: triage → scope → mitigate → verify → communicate.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• In-depth understanding of Windows operating systems and general knowledge of Unix/Linux/Mac environments.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Familiarity with cloud environments (AWS, Azure, O365, Google Cloud) and cloud IR methodologies.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Strong technical knowledge of common network protocols and design patterns (TCP/IP, HTTPS, SSH, RDP, SMB, DNS).<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Solid understanding of MITRE ATT&amp;CK and the NIST Cybersecurity Framework.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Excellent verbal and written English communication skills with a proven ability to establish relationships and clearly explain complex technical findings to executive and technical clients.</p>
<p><strong>Desirable Skills, Knowledge, and Experience</strong><br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Experience with Cloudflare products (WAF, DDoS protection, Bot Management, API Shield) or equivalent edge platforms.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Familiarity with bot fingerprinting concepts (e.g., JA3/JA4) and behavioral analysis.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Proficient in Python or Golang; capable of writing modular code or simple scripts that can be used remotely.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Familiarity with YARA and writing rules to detect similar malware samples.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Practical malware analysis experience with static, dynamic, and automated techniques.<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Understanding of source code, hex, binary, regular expressions, data correlation, and analysis (network flow and system logs).<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Familiarity with bash command-line executables to conduct static analysis and investigate Indicators of Compromise (IOCs).<br>&nbsp; &nbsp;• Experience with network protocols and devices. &nbsp;(BGP, OSPF, Routers, Firewalls, Switches)</p>
