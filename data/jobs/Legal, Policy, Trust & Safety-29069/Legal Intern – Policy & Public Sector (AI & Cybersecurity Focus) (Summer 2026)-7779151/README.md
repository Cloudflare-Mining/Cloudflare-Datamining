<p><strong>About Cloudflare's Legal Team</strong></p>
<p>The Cloudflare Legal team is at the forefront of some of the most complex and novel legal issues in technology today. You will be directly supporting our <strong>Policy</strong> and <strong>Public Sector Legal</strong> teams. These teams work collaboratively to navigate the rapidly evolving regulatory landscape, ensure Cloudflare’s compliance with stringent government standards (like FedRAMP and StateRAMP), and advocate for policies that promote a secure, privacy-respecting, and innovative Internet.</p>
<p><strong>What you’ll do</strong></p>
<p>As a Summer Legal Intern, you will dive headfirst into the intersection of law, policy, and cutting-edge technology. With a specific emphasis on the rapidly shifting AI and cybersecurity environments in North America, your day-to-day responsibilities will include:</p>
<ul>
	<li><strong>AI Policy &amp; Compliance Research:</strong> Track and analyze emerging artificial intelligence legislation and frameworks across North America, including US federal initiatives (e.g., Executive Orders, NIST AI RMF), state-level AI regulations (e.g., California, Colorado), and Canada’s Artificial Intelligence and Data Act (AIDA).</li>
	<li><strong>Cybersecurity Regulatory Tracking:</strong> Monitor updates to cybersecurity mandates such as CISA’s Secure by Design guidelines, SEC incident reporting rules, and critical infrastructure protection frameworks.</li>
	<li><strong>Public Sector Enablement:</strong> Assist the Public Sector legal team in reviewing and updating compliance materials related to FedRAMP, StateRAMP, and IRAP requirements, helping to accelerate our mission to secure government entities.</li>
	<li><strong>Drafting &amp; Advocacy:</strong> Draft legal memos, policy briefs, and internal guidance documents outlining how new North American tech regulations impact Cloudflare’s product roadmap, including our AI Gateway and zero-trust security services.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Examples of desirable skills, knowledge and experience</strong></p>
<ul>
	<li><strong>Education:</strong> Currently enrolled as a 1L or 2L in an ABA-accredited law school, with an anticipated graduation date of 2027 or 2028.</li>
	<li><strong>Tech-Forward Mindset:</strong> A strong demonstrated interest in technology law, public policy, cybersecurity, and artificial intelligence.</li>
	<li><strong>Analytical Rigor:</strong> Excellent legal research and writing skills, with the ability to distill dense regulatory text into clear, business-friendly advice.</li>
	<li><strong>Initiative:</strong> A self-starter mentality. You are comfortable navigating ambiguity and thrive in a fast-paced, highly collaborative environment.</li>
	<li>Ability to commit to a minimum 12 week summer internship.</li>
	<li>In the office 3-5 days a week in the Washington D.C. office</li>
</ul>
<p>Bonus Points if you have:</p>
